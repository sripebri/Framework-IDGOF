Geothermal Fluid
Primary Energy Source
Geothermal fluid is pumped from volcanic reservoirs at depths of 1,500–3,000 m with temperatures of 150–250°C. A ~90% capacity factor makes geothermal far more stable than intermittent sources such as solar and wind. Indonesia has a 23 GW potential—the second largest in the world.
